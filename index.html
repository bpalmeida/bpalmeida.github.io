I am a Ph.D. student at the Faculty of Sciences of the University of Lisbon. Currently, I am working at the LASIGE research unit where my research topics are, mainly, programming languages, type systems, and session types.

I am interested in programming languages in general but, right now, I am principally focused on working with session types. This interest came when I understood the importance of structuring all kinds of communication that occur in concurrent programs. One thing that arouses my interest in this matter is that we can ensure, at compile-time, that there are no communications issues. Moreover, we can also guarantee the absence of race conditions and some other properties. I consider those as essential attributes to all concurrent programming languages.

At the moment, I am working on FreeST, which is a concurrent programming language where processes communicate via message-passing. The language features bi-directional channel-based communication.
